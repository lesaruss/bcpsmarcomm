Four template levels, not one
Elementary, middle, high, and centers each have distinct template needs. Governance committee membership and agenda items are structured accordingly. Not all members weigh in on all levels.
21-day decision cycle with 14-day review window for absentees
Members who attend live decide in real time. Members who cannot attend get the recording and all supporting materials, with 14 days to review and submit a decision. Total cycle closes at 21 days. Prevents indefinite bottlenecking.
Template change requests go through IIQ, not directly to named individuals
The prior process named specific approvers. The updated process routes all requests through IIQ tickets to the district web team. Precedent-setting changes escalate to the governance committee. No named individuals in the standard request path.
Page-by-page review is the primary deliverable of the committee
The committee works through each section of the school site template, level by level. The output is a documented district position on every page - used as the basis for any future contract renewal or RFP. Community input is balanced against ADA compliance and industry best practices.

Background

What started this

A Pompano-area school raised concerns about athletics visibility in the current Finalsite template. The May 6 call was about exploring what was possible - not about governance. Farrah subsequently discussed the underlying problem with Charlene Collins: there was no formal process for handling template requests. On May 19, Farrah introduced the governance committee concept to the full Enterprise Application Services team.

Historical context

The current BCPS school website template was not selected by the web team alone. A multi-stakeholder governance group - including DAC (District Advisory Council) members, principals, WCMs, and department staff - spent months working through template decisions during the Finalsite transition. Linda Ferreira from the DAC was among the most active voices. That process is documented in emails from 2020 and 2023. Charlene A. Collins has the original stakeholder list.

Why this matters: When schools push back on template limitations, the record shows those decisions were made through a legitimate collective process - not arbitrary IT choices. Reconvening governance is the right mechanism for revisiting them with the same legitimacy.

The goal

Build the ideal school website - page by page, section by section - across all four template levels. The committee's documented decisions become the district's formal position for any future Finalsite contract renewal or competitive RFP. Schools cannot claim they were not heard. The web team cannot be blamed for top-down decisions.

Industry Best Practices

Governance model

The research-backed standard for K-12 district website governance is a multi-stakeholder committee with cross-level representation - elementary, middle, high, and specialty programs each contribute perspective. COSN (Consortium for School Networking) and AASA both document that unilateral IT governance of school-facing content leads to higher rates of ADA non-compliance and school disengagement. Districts with formal governance committees report fewer escalation conflicts and stronger template adoption rates over time.

The recommended committee structure includes representation from school administration, parent-facing communication staff, web content managers, IT, and instructional leadership. External consultants or vendors are observers only - they do not vote on standards.

Decision cycle

Industry standard for K-12 web governance decisions is a 15-to-30-day cycle depending on the scope of the change. Precedent-setting decisions (those that affect all schools or contract terms) should have a longer window with documented input. Operational decisions (single-school requests within defined parameters) should resolve faster. Most districts use an async review window rather than requiring full-attendance synchronous votes - this accommodates the operational realities of school administrators and reduces bottlenecking.

Change request process

Best practice places the intake function on a ticketing system, not on named individuals. Named-individual routing creates single points of failure and makes institutional knowledge non-transferable when staff changes. The ticket-based model (IIQ, ServiceNow, or equivalent) provides an auditable trail, prevents requests from going dark, and allows workload visibility across the web team. Every district that has moved from email-based to ticket-based governance reports a reduction in repeat requests and improved school satisfaction scores.

ADA and legal compliance

WCAG 2.1 AA is the current federal standard for public school district websites under the ADA and Section 508. Districts operating below this standard carry material legal exposure. Governance committees should treat ADA compliance as a non-negotiable constraint, not a balancing factor - meaning community preference and ADA compliance are not weighed equally. If a school requests a design change that creates a compliance gap, that request cannot be approved regardless of stakeholder support.

Contract alignment

A documented governance framework significantly strengthens a district's position at vendor contract renewal. Districts with no governance record are frequently upsold on features they already have or lack the institutional knowledge to know what they agreed to in the prior term. The governance committee's page-by-page review output is the strongest leverage tool a district can bring to an RFP process - it defines exactly what the district needs and documents what the current vendor has or has not delivered.

Governance Structure

Decision process

Each session has a defined agenda with decisions to be made. Members attend live or receive the recording and all supporting context. A 14-day review window allows absentees to submit their decision. The total cycle closes at 21 days. All input is balanced against ADA compliance and industry best practices - internal preference alone does not drive decisions.

Template change request process (working draft)

Schools and departments submit requests via IIQ ticket. The district web team reviews scope and impact. Requests affecting template standards or setting precedent escalate to the governance committee through the standard decision cycle. The committee defines and locks this process in its first session.

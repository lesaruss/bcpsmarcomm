Purpose

This is the first edition of a recurring audit of the Broward County Public Schools district website. The goal is simple: rank every department and every program and service page by real visitor traffic over the last 30 days, so that effort goes to the pages people actually use. The audit gives an objective, repeatable baseline. Each page can then be reviewed one at a time and improved for clarity, accuracy, and ease of use.

The audit runs in two halves. The computer side pulls and ranks the analytics for the department pages automatically. The district side is Sean completing the review of the public facing program and service pages. Together they produce a single prioritized list of pages to optimize, which becomes the basis for clear, page level assignments.

Methodology

Traffic is pulled from Google Analytics 4 (property 527326342, browardschools.com) for the trailing 30 days. Department figures aggregate every sub page under each department folder, so a department total reflects its full section, not a single landing page. Program and service figures come from the official Programs and Services directory, with each listed page measured against analytics. Engagement is the GA4 engagement rate. Pages on separate domains, for example Broward Virtual School and the parent payment portals, are outside this property and are not included.

Findings

Key Finding

55 of the 100 directory programs returned zero traffic. This is a tracking and linking issue, not an indication that the pages are unused. Two distinct causes, each with a clear remedy.

Finding 1: Directory links point at internal system addresses

40 programs

These directory entries link to a Finalsite system address of the form /fs/pages/####. The public reaches the same content through a friendly URL, and analytics records the visit under that friendly URL, so a lookup on the /fs/pages address finds nothing. Remedy: resolve each link to its public URL and update the program record. No content change is needed.

Finding 2: Directory links point at a section front door only

15 programs

These entries link to a single overview page that itself gets few direct visits, while the real traffic sits on sibling pages within the same section. For example, the Transportation department pulled over 22,000 sessions, but its overview page alone reads near zero. Remedy: measure the whole section folder for these programs, not just the overview page.

The Audit Process

Step 1. Automated department pass. The system ranks all department pages by traffic and refreshes on demand. This half is done and repeatable.

Step 2. District page review. Sean completes the review of the public program and service pages, resolving the tracking gaps above so every page reports real numbers.

Step 3. Page by page optimization. Working from the ranked list, each page is reviewed one at a time and checked that it is as clear, accurate, and easy to use as possible.

Step 4. Assign and improve. Specific pages are assigned with clear written instructions for what to change. Audits continue on a rolling basis and results are shared back as the work is completed.

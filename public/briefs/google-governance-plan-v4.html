Strengths
  • Google Workspace already federated with Azure AD - P-number logins work today
  • No additional licensing cost for Google Analytics (GA4 is free)
  • Google is the gold standard for web analytics - deep ecosystem, widely understood
  • FinalSite already relies on Google for all analytics - existing integration
  • Low provisioning effort: Jason confirmed bulk import is straightforward
  • Full administrative control unlocks advanced tagging, campaign tracking, and dashboards
Weaknesses
  • Six-month analytics gap (July-March during FinalSite migration) - historical data may be incomplete
  • Adds a second major ecosystem to manage alongside Microsoft
  • FinalSite code injection limits are unconfirmed - granular event tracking may require platform cooperation
  • No existing OU structure for scoping Google policy - must be built

Opportunities
  • Power BI integration: pull GA data into existing Microsoft Fabric dashboards for leadership reporting
  • FinalSite contract renewal in ~1 year - analytics capabilities a negotiating point
  • Phase 2 school-level dashboards position the district as a data-informed web governance model
  • Enrollment campaign tracking (Farrah's use case) becomes possible with full admin access
  • Consistent with how peer districts govern analytics - aligns BCPS with K-12 best practices
Threats and Risks
  • Google ecosystem expansion may trigger pressure to adopt Gemini at scale - hundreds of thousands of accounts

Platform Recommendation

Recommended - Rank 1
Google Analytics 4
GA4 is the strongest fit for BCPS's specific situation. FinalSite already relies on Google for all analytics - that existing integration removes one of the most significant implementation risks. The G Suite tenant is already federated with Azure AD, meaning district-controlled accounts can be provisioned with minimal effort. Search Console integration is native, with no additional configuration. Looker Studio provides the school and department dashboard model the group agreed on, without requiring Google accounts at the school level. At no cost, it is the only platform that addresses every district requirement without requiring new infrastructure.
Condition: a Data Processing Amendment must be executed before go-live to address the FERPA gap in the free tier. This is a standard agreement Google provides and does not require migrating to a paid product. IFS executes the DPA; the platform proceeds as planned. Risk without a DPA: medium. Risk with a DPA: low.
Rank 2
Piwik PRO
Piwik PRO is the right choice if the group decides the FERPA gap in GA4 - even with a DPA - is not acceptable. It is purpose-built for education and government, provides full district data ownership, and its compliance story requires no legal instrument to stand on. Dashboard sharing works without end-user accounts, matching the governance model the group confirmed. The gap is cost at district scale and the absence of a native Search Console integration, which means SEO reporting requires additional configuration work that GA4 handles automatically.
Recommended as: the conservative choice if FERPA certainty is the deciding factor. Best deployed if GA4 DPA negotiations stall or if legal review flags additional exposure. Evaluate a 90-day pilot before full commitment given the budget line item.
Rank 3
Azure / Microsoft Clarity
Azure is the strongest internal reporting platform on this list and the most natural fit for Power BI integration, which the district already uses. It ranks third because it is not a web analytics platform in the way GA4 is. Microsoft Clarity is a behavioral analytics and heatmap tool - it does not provide the SEO depth, Search Console integration, or campaign-level tracking the district needs for public web governance. It does not replace what is missing. It should be evaluated as a complement to whichever platform is selected, not as the primary choice.
Recommended as: a supplemental layer for internal reporting and behavioral insights, deployed alongside GA4 or Piwik PRO. Not recommended as a standalone replacement for a web analytics platform.
Decision for the Follow-Up Meeting

The group will decide between GA4 and Piwik PRO. If the DPA process is straightforward and legal has no objection, GA4 is the recommendation. If the group wants zero legal ambiguity on FERPA from day one, Piwik PRO is the path. Azure remains in play as a supplemental reporting layer under either decision. A decision that defers past the June 5 deadline extends the district's exposure on the bcps.pio@gmail.com ownership problem - the group agreed urgency is not optional.

What Other Districts Are Doing

Google Governance Best Practices in K-12: research into how other large districts govern their Google environments surfaces a consistent set of practices that BCPS does not yet have in place. These are not aspirational - they are the baseline at comparable districts.

01
IT (IFS-equivalent) Holds Primary Admin - Not Communications
Best-practice districts assign domain admin ownership to their IT or information systems division, not to communications or web staff. This is exactly the structure BCPS is moving toward: IFS as primary, OOC and AS as operational admins.
02
Organizational Unit (OU) Structure for Scoped Access
Leading districts organize their Google tenant into organizational units - separating students, staff, administrators, and departments - so that policy changes (AI features, app access, Gemini availability) can be applied at the right scope without affecting everyone. BCPS does not yet have a documented OU structure for its web analytics properties.
03
Search Console Verified via DNS, Not Personal Email
Best practice is for districts to verify Search Console via DNS TXT record through IT - this makes verification independent of any individual's account and survives staff turnover. BCPS's current verification is tied to a personal Gmail; migrating to DNS verification owned by IFS removes the single point of failure entirely.
04
Analytics Dashboards for Schools - The Jeffco Model
Jefferson County Public Schools (Colorado) built a district-wide data governance program with a dedicated program manager and department-level data stewards. Their model: IT owns access control, data stewards in each department decide who sees what within their unit. This is the Phase 2 model BCPS is targeting.
05
Recurring Google Admin Console Audits
Districts like Pickerington Local (Ohio) partner with CDW or equivalent to run periodic Google Admin Console audits - scoring their configuration against EDU best practices. BCPS's access audit (in progress as of May 19) is the right first step; formalizing it as an annual process is the mature governance posture.
06
AI Feature Policy Before Activation, Not After
Districts that deployed Gemini for Education without a formal policy first have faced parent and school board pushback. Best practice is to configure AI feature access by OU before enabling it broadly. FERPA and COPPA compliance documentation should accompany any AI feature rollout - Manuel Castaneda's team owns this workstream.

OOC
Marketing and Strategic Communications owns: Standards, Strategy, Content, Instruction

Application Services owns: Schools, Platform, Access, Technical Support
AS manages all school-level web pages from a technical standpoint. AS is the single point of contact for anything broken, misconfigured, or needing technical setup. AS implements what OOC specifies.

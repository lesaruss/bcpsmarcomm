Executive Summary

The Web Content Manager (WCM) program establishes standardized website governance across Broward County Public Schools. School principals designate WCMs who complete certification coursework and maintain compliance with BCPS website standards. This document reflects decisions from the June 9, June 24, and June 25, 2026 review sessions.

WCM Assignment and Certification Workflow

Principal-Level Assignment - Confirmed Layout (June 24)

Principals log into BCPS Central, select their school, and navigate to the Web Content Manager module. The interface is organized into two panels:

Left Panel (Step 1)
Confirm Existing WCMs
Prior year WCMs displayed for principal review and confirmation
Right Panel (Step 2)
Add Additional WCMs
Search existing users or enter new first name, last name, email
  • Panel order confirmed: left = confirm existing, right = add new (not inverted)
  • Section title: "Assign Web Content Managers" / "Add Additional Web Content Managers"
  • Principal clicks "Add" - on success, user appears in confirmed list and welcome email is triggered
  • Principals who do not need to change anything simply leave the existing WCMs as-is - no action required
  • Bulk upload feature in development: principals will confirm prior year WCMs from an uploaded file

Designation Record

Each WCM record includes a designation field that captures who approved the assignment and when. Label format: "[Principal Name] designated [WCM Name] as WCM on [date]". This is separate from certification status and covers scenarios where an assistant principal or office manager performs the entry on behalf of the principal.

  • Designated by field is separate from the certification section
  • Visible to internal admin team in read-only mode for principals
  • Captures the date of designation automatically on save

Welcome Email

A welcome email is triggered automatically when a new WCM is added. The email goes to the WCM only - the principal does not receive a copy (redundant since the principal initiated the action).

  • Email addresses WCM by first name, last name (falls back to "Web Content Manager" if name unavailable)
  • Includes link to the certification course enrollment
  • Email template formatting actively being polished (spacing, sizing); functionality confirmed working
  • Florida law disclaimer included at standard 8pt size (required)
  • Pending: Email audit trail - DB record of sender, recipient, timestamp, and content for dispute resolution to be added to scope

Certification Status Tracking

Certification uses a single linear status field (confirmed June 24). Statuses progress in one direction only - a WCM cannot be certified without first being registered.

Not Registered→
Registered→
Incomplete→
Certified
Not Registered
WCM designated by principal. Awaiting course enrollment. No action yet from WCM.
Registered
WCM enrolled in the certification course. Course in progress.
Incomplete
Working through course modules. Has not yet completed all requirements.
Certified
All modules complete. Certification achieved and valid through end date.

The status field also captures start date (enrollment) and valid-through date (certification expiry). Principals see status in read-only mode. The internal admin team updates status as WCMs progress. A bulk update report interface is planned to avoid school-by-school manual updates.

Multi-Year Audit and Compliance Management

Year Organization

A year selector dropdown at the top of the audit page controls which school year data is displayed. Checklists are stored under their actual school year - the year is not embedded in the checklist title. This keeps reports accurate when filtering by year.

  • 2025-26 incomplete items remain in the 2025-26 school year bucket
  • 2026-27 checklists are assigned to the 2026-27 year (confirmed and actioned - June 25)
  • Year selector allows admins to toggle between years without cluttering the current view
  • Approximately 60-100 schools have outstanding 2025-26 items (roughly 60 incompletes + 20+ moved to complete)

Outstanding Item Alert System

Schools with prior year incompletes should see an indicator when viewing the current year, so they are not misled into thinking they are fully compliant. A cross-year alert banner is in development.

  • Alert will appear on the current year audit page if the school has incomplete items from any prior year
  • Schools that passed the prior year see no alert
  • Report center provides district-wide cross-year visibility for the admin team

Checklist and Status Tracking

  • All schools receive an audit checklist per school year
  • Three terminal statuses: Pass, Incomplete, Fail
  • Central admin manually updates compliance status as items are verified
  • Automated status computation script in development (rules being finalized)
  • Bulk reporting and update interface planned for admin efficiency

Overview

This document establishes the governance framework for BCPS school websites managed through the Finalsite platform. It captures decisions and agreed actions from the May 6 working session with Farrah Wilson and Vanessa Deslandes, covering website template standards, the school template change request process, and the WCM certification program for school content editors.

The core governance challenge is that individual schools are requesting custom template deviations - the immediate example being Pompano Beach High School's Magnet Program focus request - while the district maintains a standardized template system under an IT contract. Any template changes require alignment across multiple stakeholders before implementation, and that alignment process was not clearly defined until this working session.

Template Change Request Process

How Schools Request Template Changes

This process was defined in the May 6 working session. All school template requests follow this path before any implementation begins.

1
School submits request to Vanessa Deslandes
Schools communicate template requests through the WCM program. The request must include the specific need, the business or educational justification, and the proposed scope of the change.
2
Vanessa reviews against current Finalsite contract scope
Vanessa determines whether the request falls within the existing contract or requires a contract amendment. If there is any question about contract scope, Charlene Collins is consulted before the request advances.
3
Farrah Wilson approves or escalates
Farrah makes the call. If the change is within contract scope and does not affect other schools or district-wide template standards, she can approve directly. If the change would affect the district-wide template or set a precedent, it escalates to the team meeting.
4
Give Tricia and Charlene a heads-up before team meeting (if escalated)
Vanessa emphasized this in the May 6 call: Charlene Collins and Tricia Allen must be informed before any team discussion. The website template contract falls under IT and impacts broader decisions. This heads-up is not optional.
5
Friday team meeting alignment
Escalated requests are presented at the Friday Enterprise Application Services meeting. Sean presents the request, stakeholders weigh in, and a decision is made. No commitments are given to the requesting school until after this step.
6
Implementation and school communication
Upon approval, Vanessa coordinates implementation with the Finalsite team. The school is notified of the outcome. If the change requires WCM re-certification for the school's editors, Vanessa handles that alongside implementation.

WCM Certification Program

Web Content Manager Certification

Vanessa Deslandes runs the WCM Certification Program, which trains and certifies school-level content editors to use the Finalsite template system correctly. The certification pilot ran May 6, 2026. Certified editors are the only staff with access to template editing functions for their school's site.

The certification program is the primary mechanism for ensuring schools use templates within approved standards. Schools that have not certified their editors cannot make template changes independently. This is enforced at the access level, not just policy.

Next steps: confirm which schools have certified editors, which are scheduled for upcoming cohorts, and which have no active certified editors and are currently relying on Vanessa's team for all content updates.

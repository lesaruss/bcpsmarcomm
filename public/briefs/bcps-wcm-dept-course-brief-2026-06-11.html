Project Summary

Broward County Public Schools currently operates a WCM Certification course in Canvas for school-level Web Content Managers. The goal of this project is to adapt and expand that course into a parallel Department version - one that addresses the distinct responsibilities, content standards, and governance expectations of BCPS department WCMs.

Department WCMs serve a fundamentally different function than school WCMs. Department pages are the source of truth for district-wide policies, forms, and guidance used by 200+ schools, thousands of families, and internal staff. This course reflects that elevated responsibility.

All source content has been gathered, reviewed, and mapped to modules. The course is ready for content writing beginning June 11, 2026.

Key Decisions

12-Module structure confirmed
The Department course will have 12 modules plus Final Assignments, up from 6 in the Schools version. Modules 1 and 9-12 adapt existing content with terminology updates. Modules 2-8 are new or fully rewritten for department framing. This structure ensures full coverage of both Finalsite CMS skills and BCPS department content governance standards.
Outline-first approach: confirm before writing
Content writing begins only after outline approval. This brief is the outline. Once approved, modules will be written in order: Modules 1-2 on Day 1, Modules 3-8 on Day 2, Modules 9-12 and mastery checks on Day 3.
Source material fully identified - three documents plus live site
All module content has confirmed sources: Department WCM Standards.docx (Modules 3-8), Department WCM Guidelines.docx (Modules 10-11), the live Standards and Guidelines sub-pages on browardschools.com (supplemental detail for Modules 3-8), and the existing Canvas course (Modules 1 and 9-12 adaptation base).

BOSS Analysis

BOSS = Best Practice first, Outcome first, Scientific method, then Simplify. Applied to the question: Is this course the right approach for providing standards training to BCPS department WCMs?

B
Best Practice First

Best practice for large-district WCM training (confirmed through research): tiered certification with mandatory ADA compliance; LMS delivery with assessments and badge awards; separation of CMS tool skills from content governance standards; role-specific content for different site types; regular audit cycles built into the curriculum.

BCPS already meets all of these criteria. The additional best practice finding: no other comparable district has a department-specific version of their WCM training. Most programs treat all content managers as equivalent. BCPS recognizing that department WCMs operate at a higher governance level - serving as source of truth for 200+ schools - is itself best-practice differentiation.

One gap vs. best practice: NYC DOE has an explicit video accessibility certification tier (captioning, screencasts). BCPS should consider adding this as a future module. Not blocking for this build.

O
Outcome First (Top-Down)

The outcome: A BCPS department WCM who completes this course can independently maintain a department website that passes a BCPS content audit, meets ADA compliance, and functions as a reliable district source of truth.

Measured by: WCM earns the certification badge AND passes a Phase 1 audit review with no critical findings. Working backward from that outcome confirms the 12-module structure is correct - every module maps to an auditable competency. No module is decorative. Content quality, navigation, layout, post boards, resource elements, and style are all audit categories in the existing QA checklist, which means the course structure and the audit rubric are now aligned.

S
Scientific Method (Hypothesis, Test, Iterate)

Hypothesis: Department WCMs trained on department-specific content and governance framing will perform better on content audits and require fewer corrections than department WCMs trained on the school-focused version.

Test: Track audit scores and common failure categories for department WCMs before and after the course launches. Compare against school WCM audit performance as a baseline.

Iterate: Modules where audit failures remain high after certification indicate content gaps. The per-module mastery checks (Modules 3-8) will surface knowledge gaps before WCMs go live. Update module content each semester based on the most common audit failure patterns.

S
Simplify (Cut Against the Evidence)

What to keep: The 12-module structure is justified by the audit - each module maps to a real competency gap. Do not collapse modules.

What to cut or defer: Modules 9 and 12 (ADA and Building Accessible Webpages) carry over as-is - no rewrite needed, evidence shows ADA requirements are universal. The Finalsite modules (10-11) need integration of the Guidelines doc but are not rebuilds. Video accessibility is a future module, not a Day 1 requirement. Plain language writing could be folded into Module 4 (Content Quality) rather than added as a standalone - this keeps scope tight.

Net result: New writing effort is concentrated in Modules 2-8. Everything else is light editing. This is the right scope allocation.

Competitive Benchmarking - How BCPS Stacks Up

Research conducted June 11, 2026. Comparable large public school districts with identifiable WCM training programs.

NYC Department of Education (1.1M students - largest district in the US)
Three-tier Digital Accessibility Certification via OTIS/Teq: Digital Accessibility Basics for All Staff (3.5 hrs, 10 modules), Webmasters and Application Developers (1.6 hrs, 4 modules), and Video Creators (1.5 hrs, 3 modules). Mandatory for all webmasters. Badge issued. Hosted on a third-party platform (OTIS/Teq), not Canvas. Coverage is almost entirely accessibility-focused. No CMS how-to training. No content governance or department vs. school differentiation. No layout, navigation, post boards, or resource element standards.
BCPS ADVANTAGE: BCPS course is significantly more comprehensive. NYC covers only ADA. BCPS covers ADA plus CMS skills, content quality, layout, navigation, post boards, resource elements, and style - plus both school and department versions.
Houston ISD (200,000+ students - second largest single-district in US)
HISD Web Services manages 280+ campus sites and previously provided webmaster training. As of research date, webmaster training is paused during a platform transition. No public certification program or badge system identified. Department vs. school distinction not publicly addressed.
BCPS ADVANTAGE: BCPS has an active, structured program. HISD currently has none.
Los Angeles Unified (LAUSD, 400,000+ students)
No publicly identifiable WCM certification or standards training program found. LAUSD uses a variety of CMS platforms across schools and departments. Web governance is decentralized.
BCPS ADVANTAGE: BCPS has a centralized, standardized WCM program. LAUSD does not.
National Standard - School Webmasters / Finalsite Community
Finalsite's own training covers platform how-to but not district content governance. No Finalsite-delivered certification for department vs. school distinctions exists. The Finalsite community blog covers governance roles and permission tiers but does not publish curriculum or certifications. School Webmasters (a national organization) offers website accessibility training but not a district-level governance certification.
BCPS ADVANTAGE: No comparable district-delivered, CMS-plus-governance certification on the Finalsite platform exists publicly. BCPS is building what may be the most comprehensive K-12 WCM certification in the country for a Finalsite district.
Gap vs. Best Practice: Video Accessibility
NYC DOE's Video Creators certification (captioning, translated captions, accessible screencasts) addresses a gap BCPS does not currently cover. As departments publish more video content, this will become a compliance issue. WCAG 2.1 AA requires captions and audio descriptions for all pre-recorded video content. Federal deadline for large districts was April 24, 2026.
GAP - FUTURE MODULE: Add a Video Accessibility module in a future course iteration. Not blocking for the current build, but flag for the next course update cycle.

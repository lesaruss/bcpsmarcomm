Operational Results

Smart Objectives
Three objectives assessed at end of year
H - Highly Effective
Objective 1
Develop a roadmap for a cross-departmental Web Community that includes training plans, resource guides, communication protocols, and a support system to improve collaboration and content quality by June 30, 2025.
Delivered: Finalsite training roadmap complete. Web Community framework live and in active use by the entire District Web Team.
The Web Community infrastructure was designed from the ground up to be scalable and sustainable. Training materials, resource guides, and tiered support protocols were documented and distributed across all participating departments. The Finalsite platform rollout was coordinated centrally and successfully adopted district-wide, establishing a consistent content management foundation that continues to operate post-launch.
Objective 2
Collaborate with 100% of BCPS departments to document and implement website migration and maintenance best practices by June 30, 2025, ensuring consistency, accessibility, and brand alignment.
Exceeded: Full department reach achieved via a three-channel support model - Newsletters, 1-on-1 sessions, and Hot Labs held three times per week.
Rather than a single-format rollout, Sean designed a multi-format support structure that met department Web Content Managers (WCMs) at every skill level. Hot Labs provided open drop-in troubleshooting. One-on-one sessions offered tailored guidance for departments with complex migration needs. Newsletters kept all WCMs informed on standards updates and platform changes. This approach ensured no department was left behind and produced measurable progress across every site in scope.
Objective 3
Audit, organize, and update all web pages for the Office of Communications and Legislative Affairs by June 30, 2025, ensuring accuracy, ADA compliance, and alignment with district messaging standards.
On track: Full completion confirmed for June 30, 2025 with ADA compliance and messaging alignment verified throughout.
The audit process applied a structured review methodology covering content accuracy, ADA compliance (WCAG 2.0 AA standards), broken link remediation, and brand alignment. Work was sequenced to ensure the most high-traffic and public-facing pages were prioritized. The Office of Communications and Legislative Affairs both reflect the updated district messaging standards as of the end of the school year.
Supervisor Comment (Farrah Wilson)

"Sean successfully met each of his SMART objectives, including the development of a detailed roadmap for a cross-departmental web community. His strategic planning, coordination, and follow-through were instrumental in documenting and implementing web migrations that led to the successful launch of the new website."

He elevated the function of the District Webmaster role from technical execution to organizational leadership, creating systems that will sustain the district's web quality well beyond this evaluation cycle.
